# MeetingHub-4
# Hardware Architecture Document (HAD-001)

Version: 1.0
Status: Final — architecture implemented and validated in hardware


# 1. Overview

MeetingHub-4 is an analog device intended to manage audio for four laptops using TRRS CTIA headset connections.

The device shall:

- receive audio from four laptops;
- mix the signals in stereo;
- provide monitoring through a single headset;
- route the headset microphone to only one selected laptop.

# 3. Design Philosophy

The device shall be transparent to the laptops.

The computer must interpret the device as a directly connected TRRS CTIA headset.

The following will not be used:

- drivers;
- USB audio communication;
- digital processing.


# 4. Subsystems


## 4.1 TRRS Input

Each laptop will be connected through a metallic panel-mount P2 TRRS CTIA connector.

Signals:

- Tip       = Left Audio
- Ring 1    = Right Audio
- Ring 2    = Ground
- Sleeve    = Microphone


## 4.2 Audio System

Architecture:

- active stereo mixer;
- individual volume control;
- master control;
- dedicated headset amplifier.


## 4.3 Microphone System

Requirements:

- preservation of the microphone bias voltage provided by the laptop;
- transparent switching;
- absence of active electronics on the microphone line.

Initial solution:

- switching via signal relays;
- normally open contact;
- low contact resistance;
- no active components between headset and laptop.


## 4.4 Power Supply

Input:

USB-C 5V.

The power supply will be isolated from the laptop's audio line.

The circuit shall prevent noise from returning to the TRRS inputs.

It will power:

- active mixer;
- headphone amplifier;
- control circuit.


# 5. Noise Considerations

The design shall take into account:

- star grounding;
- separation between audio and power supply;
- power supply filters;
- interference protection.


# 6. Main Components (provisional)

- Audio operational amplifier

- Dedicated IC

Microphone:
- Signal relays

Connectors:
- Metallic P2 TRRS connectors
